Currency

The name of the Vietnamese currency is đồng. The smaller monetary units xu (1/10 of Vietnamese đồng) and hào (1/100 of Vietnamese đồng) are rarely used. The smallest banknote denomination is 100 đồng, and the biggest is 500,000 đồng (Năm trăm nghìn).

Colours

Vietnamese only has one word to express the colours blue and green, xanh. When there is a need to specify between the two, a description complement must be added. For example, xanh nước biển (xanh as the sea water) is blue and xanh lá cây (xanh as a leaf) is green.

  • Áo chị Lan màu gì? What colour is Lan's dress?
  • Màu xanh. Xanh (green or blue).
  • Xanh gì? Which xanh?
  • Xanh lá cây. Green (xanh as a tree leaf).

Shopping

The most popular place for doing everyday shopping (and in some areas the only place) is a market. There you can buy everything you need - from food, fruit and vegetables, spices, to crockery, chopsticks, electrical goods, baskets, animals, clothes, and many more. The market is not only a place to shop but it is also a place to meet up with neighbours and exchange the latest news and gossip.

The largest market in Hanoi is Đồng Xuân market, in Hue it is Đồng Ba market, and in Saigon it is Bến Thành market. Strolling around the market is one of the most enjoyable ways to acquaint yourself with local produce and learn the names of various fruit, vegetables and dishes simply by asking the stall holders. And of course, it is the place to improve your bargaining skills.
